I'm currently in the process of devolping a new website for my photography to be bought and displayed and it's getting closer and closer to being finished. That being said, I decided to take a minute and write this page to take over the old site. Below what I'm writing you will see a couple images that are for sale right now. If you click on them a magic window will appear and show you options for ordering, and you will be directed to Paypal to complete the order. But first, read why I've made this page...

My dad was diagnosed with prostate cancer in 2008 and passed away in August of 2013. He loved the LIVESTRONG Foundation and everything it stood for and was a Livestrong Leader for a good number of years. Everything he owned, I think, was either Livestrong or yellow. He passed bracelets out to everyone he came across and told them to look into the foundation for support just as he had. The Livestrong community to him was a second family, which is why I made this page. All proceeds of the prints displayed below will go to the LIVESTRONG Foundation in memory of my father, Jon Colbert.

I'll only be donating ALL proceeds until the new site is finished and up and running, but after it's up I will still give 25% of proceeds on these images to the foundation. It's my way of giving back to the charity that has done so much for so many. And if you have time and haven't seen this video about him, I suggest you check it out.

All photographs are printed on archival paper with a lustre finish and are matted on white (except the 16"x20", which is for the print only).

Thank you for taking the time to read this. Please follow me on Facebook for updates on how much has been raised and status of the new website. Also, be sure to check out my other prints available through my Etsy shop right here. Jon Colbert's Verse with Yellow Flowers

Verse 6 in this chapter starts "For I am already being poured out like a drink offering, and the time for my departure is near." Which is a pretty strong start to my dads favorite verse; "I have fought the good fight, I have won the race, I have kept the faith." Which, I think describes him to a "T". He was always one to help others and witnessed to countless strangers along the way. The end, however, is my favorite part of all, which reads: "Now there is in store for me the crown of righteousness, which the Lord, the righteous Judge, will award me on that day-and not only to me, but also to all who have longed for his appearing." It is because of this and my faith in Jesus, that I know I will see my dad again. Knowing that he is in a better place and no longer suffering and the fact that I will someday see him again, makes his passing, in a way, a good thing because I know where he is and that he isn't completely gone.
