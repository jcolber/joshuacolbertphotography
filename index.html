I'm currently in the process of devolping a new website for my photography to be bought and displayed and it's getting closer and closer to being finished. That being said, I decided to take a minute and write this page to take over the old site. Below what I'm writing you will see a couple images that are for sale right now. If you click on them a magic window will appear and show you options for ordering, and you will be directed to Paypal to complete the order. But first, read why I've made this page...

My dad was diagnosed with prostate cancer in 2010 and passed away 3 years ago come August. He loved the LIVESTRONG Foundation and everything it stood for and was a Livestrong Leader for a good number of years. Everything he owned, I think, was either Livestrong, or yellow. He passed bracelets out to everyone he came across and told them to look into the foundation for support just as he had. The Livestrong community to him was a second family, which is why I made this page. All proceeds will go to the LIVESTRONG Foundation in memory of my father, Jon Colbert.

I'll only be donating ALL proceeds until the new site is finished and up and running, but after it's up I will still give 25% of proceeds on these images to the foundation. It's my way of giving back to the charity that has done so much for so many. And if you have time and haven't seen this video about him, I suggest you check it out.

Thank you for taking the time to read this, and feel free to check out some of my other, more boring links at the top of this page.

Sunset in the Wasatch

One of my favorite silhouette pictures taken was when we were headed down from Lake Blanche and the sun was setting on us. I don't think I've ever finished a hike while it was still light out, but headlamps and cooler temperatures make it that much more fun. Lake Blanche is an awesome destination, and if you're ever in Salt Lake City, its worth the 20 minute drive, and couple hour hike to go see.

Skyline Arch in Moab, Utah

On a spur of the moment trip down to Moab, we stopped at Arches National Park and explored some of the arches around there. Hundreds of arches can be found in this park which makes it a fabulous destination. Especially when you only live 4 hours away and can drive down willy nilly on a Friday night and camp on BLM land beneath the stars without anyone else around you. No cell phone coverage, and being in the middle of nowhere is what camping is all about.
